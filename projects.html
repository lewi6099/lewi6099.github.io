STAT 320: Exploring the Factors That Influence Quality of Life

  • Project from STAT 320, Applied Regression Analysis
  • During this project I worked with a group of 2 other students to analyze what factors influence a person's self-perceived quality of life
  • Factors that were tested include marital status, siblings, children, the age of the respondnent when they had their first born child, years of completed education, years of father's education, years of mother's education, amount of people earning an income in the respondent's household, political party, job satisfcation, if the respondent owns a gun, and income
  • Respondents were then asked to rate their quality of life on a sclae of 1 - 10
  • Results of the study showed that the only variables that were useful in determining quality of life were marital status, education, and income
  • The image shows these variables being shown in the regression analysis output
